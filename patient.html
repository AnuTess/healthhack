Your HbA1c Level 6% What is HbA1C? The term HbA1c refers to glycated haemoglobin. It develops when excess sugar binds to the hemoglobin protein in your red blood cells. HbA1c is also referred to as hemoglobin A1c or simply A1c. What do my HbA1c Blood Test Results Mean? Elevated HbA1c levels mean that blood sugar levels have been too high for the past three months (the lifespan of a red blood cell). This test can be used to indicate diabetes or pre-diabetes. Your HbA1c Level = 6% Normal: 4% to 5.6% Prediabetes: 5.7% to 6.5% Diabetes: Over 6.5% While the blood glucose level is the concentration of glucose in your blood at a single point in time (the very moment of the test), the HbA1c test serves as an overall marker of what your average levels are over a period of three months. The higher the HbA1c, the greater the risk of developing or worsening diabetes-related complications. How Do I Lower my HbA1c Levels? Exercise. Try to get at least 30 minutes of exercise five days a week. Find something you enjoy doing that gets your body moving â€” take your dog for a walk, play a sport with a friend, or ride a stationary bike indoors or a regular bike outdoors. Eat a balanced diet. You can load up on non-starchy vegetables, but be mindful of serving sizes when eating fruits, lean proteins, fats, and complex carbohydrates like bread, potatoes, and other starches. Avoid processed foods, especially sugary sodas and fruit juices, as much as possible. Be Mindful of Meal Frequency and Portion Size. Skipping meals, letting too much time pass between meals, or eating too much or too often can cause your blood sugar levels to fall and rise too much.
